Sound Familiar?

Signs You Need HVAC Help

Any of these happening? Stop Googling and call us. We'll tell you what's wrong and what it costs — before touching anything.

AC Running But Not Cooling

Running constantly but the house stays hot. Usually a refrigerant issue, bad capacitor, or dirty coil — all fixable same day.

Weak Airflow From Vents

Barely any air coming out. Could be a clogged filter, failing blower motor, or blocked ductwork. We diagnose and fix on the spot.

Water Leaking Around Unit

Pooling water near your indoor unit means a clogged drain line or frozen evaporator coil. Left alone it causes water damage.

Banging or Grinding Noises

Unusual sounds mean something mechanical is failing. Don't wait — a $150 part today prevents a $1,500 compressor tomorrow.

Electric Bill Suddenly Spiked

A system that's losing efficiency runs longer to hit the same temperature. A tune-up or refrigerant recharge usually fixes it.

Heat Not Working in Winter

Furnace won't ignite, heat pump stuck in cooling mode, thermostat unresponsive. We handle all of it, same day in Philadelphia.

FAQ

HVAC Questions Philadelphia Customers Ask

Do you offer same-day HVAC repair in Philadelphia?

Yes. We prioritize same-day service for HVAC emergencies across Philadelphia, Bucks County, Delaware County, Montgomery County, and surrounding areas. Call (803) 221-7013 and we'll tell you our earliest available time.

What HVAC brands do you service?

All of them — Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, York, Goodman, American Standard, Bryant, and more. If it blows air, we service it.

Is a heat pump worth it in Philadelphia?

Yes — especially now. Heat pumps qualify for up to $2,000 in federal tax credits. Philadelphia's climate is well-suited for heat pumps. They heat and cool in one system, and they're 2–3x more efficient than gas furnaces. We'll walk you through the math before you decide.

How long does an HVAC repair take?

Most standard repairs — capacitors, contactors, refrigerant, thermostat issues — are completed in 1–3 hours on the same visit. Compressor replacements or full system installs are typically scheduled separately and take a full day.

What if my system is too old to repair?

We'll tell you honestly. If a repair costs more than 50% of a new system's price and your unit is over 10 years old, we'll say so. We'd rather lose a repair job and earn a customer for life than oversell you a band-aid fix.
