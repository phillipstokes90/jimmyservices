Carpentry Problems Philadelphia Homeowners Face

Older homes and outdoor structures take a beating from the weather. Here's what we fix most.

Rotting or Warped Deck Boards

Soft spots and warped boards are a safety issue, not just cosmetic. We repair or fully rebuild decks to code.

Sagging or Leaning Fence

Rotted posts and shifted panels. We reset posts properly with concrete footings so it doesn't happen again.

Outdated or Damaged Cabinets

Custom cabinets, refacing, or built-ins for kitchens, bathrooms, and basements — built to fit your space exactly.

Warped or Squeaky Flooring

Hardwood, laminate, and engineered flooring installation and repair. We fix subfloor issues, not just the surface.

Missing or Damaged Trim

Baseboards, crown molding, door and window casing. Small details that make a room look finished — or unfinished.

Door or Window Won't Close Right

Framing has shifted or was never square to begin with. We reframe and rehang so it operates correctly.

FAQ

Carpentry Questions Philadelphia Homeowners Ask

Why can't you give me a price over the phone for carpentry?

Carpentry cost depends heavily on materials, existing condition, and project size — all things we need to see in person. On-site quotes are free and come with no obligation.

Do you build custom decks and fences?

Yes — pressure-treated, cedar, and composite decking, plus wood and vinyl fencing, built or repaired to your specifications.

Can you match existing trim or cabinetry?

In most cases yes. We can match or closely match existing profiles and finishes so additions and repairs blend in.

Do you handle both repairs and new builds?

Yes — from a single rotted deck board to a full deck rebuild, cabinet install, or flooring project.

Do you work on commercial carpentry projects?

Yes, we take on commercial finish carpentry and framing work for offices, retail, and other business properties.
