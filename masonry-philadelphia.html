Sound Familiar?

Masonry Problems That Get Worse Every Winter

Freeze-thaw cycles are hard on Philadelphia masonry. Small cracks become big repairs if you wait.

Cracked Steps or Stoop

A trip hazard and a liability. We rebuild or resurface concrete and stone steps so they're safe and code-compliant.

Crumbling Mortar Joints

Repointing failing mortar before water gets behind the brick prevents much bigger structural repairs later.

Leaning or Bowing Retaining Wall

A wall under pressure from soil and water will fail eventually. We rebuild with proper drainage so it stays put.

Chimney Brick Spalling

Flaking, crumbling brick on your chimney lets water into the structure. We repair and repoint before it spreads.

Uneven or Sinking Walkway

Settled pavers or slabs create trip hazards and pooling water. We regrade and relay for proper drainage.

Water Pooling Near Foundation

Failed grading or a cracked patio slab near the house often points back to masonry, not just drainage.

FAQ

Masonry Questions Philadelphia Homeowners Ask

Why can't you give me a price over the phone?

Masonry cost depends on material, existing damage, and square footage — all things we need to see in person to quote accurately. On-site quotes are free and come with no obligation.

What time of year is best for masonry work?

Spring through fall is ideal — mortar needs temperatures consistently above freezing to cure properly. We can assess and schedule work year-round, but pouring or repointing in winter needs special conditions.

Do you repair chimneys as well as build them?

We focus on repair and repointing — spalling brick, cracked crowns, and deteriorating mortar joints — for existing chimneys across Philadelphia.

Can you match existing brick or stone?

In most cases yes. We source matching or close-matching brick and stone for repairs so the patch blends with your existing structure.

Do you handle both residential and commercial masonry?

Yes — homes, storefronts, and commercial properties throughout Philadelphia and surrounding counties.
