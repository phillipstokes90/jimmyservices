FAQ

EV Charger Questions Philadelphia Residents Ask

What's the difference between Level 1 and Level 2 charging?

Level 1 uses a standard 120V outlet and gives you about 3–5 miles of range per hour. Level 2 uses a 240V dedicated circuit (the same voltage as your dryer) and gives you 25–35 miles per hour. Most EV owners find Level 1 completely impractical for daily driving. Level 2 fills your car overnight from nearly empty.

Does my electrical panel need to be upgraded?

Many Philadelphia homes can handle a Level 2 charger without a panel upgrade — we'll check your panel capacity as the first step. If your panel is at or near capacity, we'll give you an honest quote for what's needed before starting any work.

Do you install EV chargers for all electric car brands?

Yes. Tesla, Ford, GM (Chevy, Cadillac), Rivian, Hyundai/Kia, BMW, Audi, Volkswagen, Nissan, Honda, Volvo — any EV that uses a Level 2 J1772 connector, and any Tesla with their proprietary Wall Connector. We're experienced with all of them.

How long does the installation take?

Most standard residential installations take 2–4 hours. We complete same-day in the vast majority of cases. You'll be charging that night.

Can I claim a tax credit for EV charger installation in Philadelphia?

Yes. The Inflation Reduction Act provides a 30% federal tax credit on both the equipment and installation costs, up to $1,000 for residential installations. We provide all documentation needed for filing IRS Form 8911. Consult your tax advisor for your specific situation.
