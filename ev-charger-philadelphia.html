FAQ

EV Charger Questions Philadelphia Residents Ask

What's the difference between Level 1 and Level 2 charging?

Level 1 uses a standard 120V outlet — 3–5 miles of range per hour. Level 2 uses a 240V dedicated circuit (same voltage as your dryer) — 25–35 miles per hour. Most EV owners find Level 1 completely impractical for daily driving. Level 2 fills your car overnight from nearly empty.

Does my electrical panel need to be upgraded?

Many Philadelphia homes can handle a Level 2 charger without a panel upgrade. We check your panel capacity as the first step on-site. If your panel is near capacity, we give you an honest quote for what's needed before starting any work.

Do you install EV chargers for all electric car brands?

Yes — Tesla, Ford, GM, Rivian, Hyundai/Kia, BMW, Audi, Volkswagen, Nissan, Honda, Volvo, and any EV using a Level 2 J1772 connector or Tesla Wall Connector.

How long does the installation take?

Most standard residential installations take 2–4 hours. We complete same-day in the vast majority of cases. You'll be charging that night.

Can I claim a tax credit for EV charger installation?

Yes. The Inflation Reduction Act provides a 30% federal tax credit on equipment and installation costs, up to $1,000 for residential (IRS Form 8911). We provide all documentation needed. Consult your tax advisor for your specific situation.
