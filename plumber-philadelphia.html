FAQ

Plumbing Questions Philadelphia Homeowners Ask

What should I do if I have a burst pipe?

Turn off your main water shutoff valve immediately — usually in your basement or utility area. Then call us at (803) 221-7013. While you wait, mop up standing water if safe and move valuables away from the wet area.

Do you service older Philadelphia rowhomes?

Absolutely — most of our work is in older Philly housing stock. We're very familiar with galvanized pipes, cast iron drain lines, and older construction quirks. We know what to look for and how to fix it without tearing up your home unnecessarily.

Can you replace my water heater same day?

Often yes. We stock common tank water heater sizes on the van. Tankless units typically require a next-day scheduled install. Call and we'll tell you based on what we have available.

Do you handle commercial plumbing?

Yes. Licensed for commercial work in Philadelphia County. Grease traps, commercial water heaters, backflow prevention, floor drains, multi-unit residential buildings. Call for a quote.

Do you give free estimates?

For standard jobs, we can often give a phone estimate. For complex or emergency jobs, we charge a standard service call which is applied toward the repair if you proceed.
