FAQ

Plumbing Questions Philadelphia Homeowners Ask

What should I do if I have a burst pipe?

Turn off your main water shutoff valve immediately — it's usually in your basement or utility area. Then call us at (803) 221-7013. While you wait: mop up standing water if safe to do so, and move valuables away from the wet area. Don't use electrical outlets near the water.

Do you service older Philadelphia rowhomes?

Absolutely — most of our work is in older Philly housing stock. We're very familiar with galvanized pipes, cast iron drain lines, and the quirks of older construction. We know what to look for and how to fix it without tearing up your home unnecessarily.

Can you replace my water heater same day?

Often yes. We stock common tank water heater sizes in the van. Tankless units typically require a scheduled install (next day in most cases). Call us and we'll tell you what we can do based on what we have available.

Do you handle commercial plumbing?

Yes. We're licensed for commercial work in Philadelphia County and surrounding areas. Grease traps, commercial water heaters, backflow prevention, floor drains, and multi-unit residential buildings. Call for a quote.
